🛡️ Disaster Preparedness

Know what to do before, during, and after a disaster

Prepare for Each Hazard

🔥

Fire

  • Install smoke alarms on every floor
  • Know two ways out of every room
  • Practice fire drills twice a year
  • Never leave cooking unattended
  • Keep a fire extinguisher handy
🌊

Flood

  • Know your barangay's evacuation routes
  • Never cross flooded roads on foot
  • Keep documents in waterproof bags
  • Stock 3-day water and food supply
  • Follow LGU flood advisories
🌀

Typhoon

  • Secure loose items outside your home
  • Charge all devices before landfall
  • Stay indoors during Signal #2 and above
  • Prepare a go-bag ready to grab
  • Monitor PAGASA updates regularly
⛰️

Landslide

  • Avoid slopes during heavy rain
  • Watch for cracks in the ground
  • Listen for unusual sounds (cracking)
  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Report warning signs to your barangay
🏥

First Aid Basics

  • Learn CPR and basic first aid
  • Keep a first aid kit at home
  • Know the nearest hospital
  • Store prescription meds (7-day supply)
  • Learn how to treat burns & wounds
📋

Family Plan

  • Designate a family meeting point
  • Keep emergency contacts written down
  • Assign roles to each family member
  • Practice drills with children
  • Register with your barangay

Before a Disaster Strikes

1

Make a Family Emergency Plan

Discuss evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication strategies with your family.

2

Build an Emergency Kit (Go-Bag)

Pack essentials like water, food, meds, flashlight, radio, and important documents.

3

Know Your Evacuation Center

Locate your barangay's designated evacuation center and the best route to reach it.

4

Stay Informed

Follow official channels: PAGASA, PHIVOLCS, NDRRMC, and your local government for updates.

5

Protect Important Documents

Store copies of birth certificates, land titles, and IDs in waterproof bags or cloud storage.
